## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Complete Your Founder-Market Fit (Part A, Step 4)

### Step 2: Part B - Adversarial Analysis

#### B.1: Adversarial AI Critique

**Action:** Consolidate your outputs from Part A (Value Prop, Target Markets, Competitors) and ask AI to critique them.

**Suggested Prompt:**
```
Critique my project's value proposition, target market, and competitive analysis:

[Paste your consolidated Value Proposition, Target Markets, and Competitor sections]

Why might this not be a true blue ocean? What types of competitors might have been overlooked? What are the biggest risks to product-market fit? What assumptions am I making that could be wrong?
```

**Documentation Steps:**
1. Copy the exact prompt you used
2. Paste the full AI critique output
3. Write your initial analysis of its validity
4. Reflect on what weaknesses are valid vs. overstated

#### B.2: Refine Project Definition

**Action:** Based on the adversarial critique, refine your sections.

**For each section (Value Prop, Target Markets, Competitors):**
1. Document what changed
2. Explain why you made the change (or why you didn't)
3. Show before/after if significant

**Example Format:**
```
**Changes Made to Value Proposition:**
- Before: [original text]
- After: [refined text]
- Rationale: [why you changed it - e.g., "AI correctly pointed out that we overemphasized affordability and underemphasized the programmable controls. Added more detail about on-chain enforcement."]
```

#### B.3: Critique & Refine FMF

**Action:** Have AI critique your FMF, then refine it.

**Suggested Prompt:**
```
Critique my founder-market fit:

[Paste your FMF paragraph]

What makes it potentially weak? How could I strengthen my positioning? What gaps exist between my background and what's needed to succeed with this project?
```

**Documentation Steps:**
1. Document the AI critique
2. Analyze which weaknesses are valid
3. Refine your FMF based on feedback
4. Document rationale for changes

## Tips for Success

1. **Be Honest in Adversarial Analysis:** The point is to find weaknesses, not defend your idea. The stronger your critique, the better your final proposal.

2. **Document Everything:** Even if something seems minor, document it. The Process Appendix should show your complete journey.

3. **Show Your Thinking:** In reflections, explain not just what you did, but why you did it and what you learned.

4. **Cite Sources:** In the Research Sources appendix, include URLs, dates accessed, and key findings.

5. **Time Management:** 
   - Complete FMF first (needs your personal input)
   - Then do adversarial analysis (can be done in one session)
   - Then refine based on critique
   - Finally compile PDF

## Questions to Consider During Adversarial Analysis

**Value Proposition:**
- Is the problem real or perceived?
- Are there existing workarounds that are "good enough"?
- Is the value proposition clear and differentiated?

**Target Markets:**
- Are the markets large enough?
- Can you reach them cost-effectively?
- Are they willing to pay $50/month?
- Is the intersection of "SME" + "crypto user" + "needs controls" large enough?

**Competitors:**
- What if Squads Protocol adds programmable controls?
- What if enterprise solutions lower prices?
- What if basic wallets add multi-sig features?
- Are you overlooking any competitors?

**Founder-Market Fit:**
- Do you have the technical skills to build this?
- Do you have access to the target market?
- Can you compete with established players?
- What's your unfair advantage?
